Home

I am a mechanical engineer and researcher focusing on metal additive manufacturing and data-driven process optimization. My work combines Laser Powder Bed Fusion (L-PBF), laser cladding / Directed Energy Deposition (DED), and machine learning to improve surface quality, microstructure, and performance of metallic components.

My experience spans experimental research, finite element analysis, and predictive modelling of process–structure–property relationships in advanced manufacturing. I work on practical challenges in aerospace, marine and offshore, and medical devices, where reliable and high-performance materials are essential.

I collaborate with academic and industrial partners to develop robust process windows, intelligent monitoring strategies, and surface engineering solutions that enable the next generation of metal additive manufacturing.

Research

My research aims to advance metal additive manufacturing by integrating process understanding, numerical modelling, and intelligent data analysis. Key themes include:

  • Process–structure–property relationships in L-PBF and DED/laser cladding.
  • Control of surface roughness, porosity, and microstructure in metal AM parts.
  • Machine learning models for predicting surface and mechanical properties.
  • Physics informed machine learning models for predicting Quality of Metal Additive Manufacturing
  • Development of surface-hardening and corrosion-resistant coatings for industrial applications.
  • Sustainable AM strategies, including efficient powder usage and energy-aware processing.
